Methodology

FloodKH uses a multi-factor scoring system to assess flood risk across 25 monitoring locations in Cambodia. This page describes the classification system, data sources, and known limitations.

Overview

Each location is evaluated every 6 hours using the latest meteorological and hydrological data. A composite risk score (0–100) is calculated from multiple weighted features, then mapped to a risk level classification.

Risk Level Thresholds

Risk Level Score Range Description
LOW 0 – 29 Normal conditions. No significant flood risk expected.
MODERATE 30 – 49 Elevated conditions. Localized flooding possible in low-lying areas.
HIGH 50 – 74 Significant flood risk. Flooding expected in vulnerable areas.
CRITICAL 75 – 100 Severe flood risk. Widespread flooding likely. Immediate action recommended.

Scoring Features

The risk score is computed as a weighted sum of the following features, each normalized to a 0–100 sub-score:

1. Rainfall — 24 Hour (Weight: 15%)

2. Rainfall — 3 Day Accumulation (Weight: 15%)

Cumulative rainfall over 72 hours captures sustained precipitation events that saturate the ground and overwhelm drainage systems. Scoring follows a similar tiered approach scaled for 3-day totals.

3. Rainfall — 7 Day Accumulation (Weight: 10%)

Longer-term rainfall context. High 7-day totals indicate already-wet conditions prone to additional flooding.

4. Rainfall Forecast — 3 Day (Weight: 15%)

Predicted rainfall from weather models for the next 72 hours. This is the primary forward-looking indicator, allowing the system to warn of impending risk increases.

5. Soil Moisture (Weight: 15%)

Soil Moisture (%)Points

Saturated soil cannot absorb additional rainfall, leading to increased surface runoff and flood risk.

6. River Discharge Ratio (Weight: 20%)

Current river discharge compared to the historical average for the same period. This is the highest-weighted feature because river levels are the most direct indicator of flooding.

7. Elevation Modifier (Weight: 10%)

Lower elevations are more vulnerable to flooding. Locations below 15m receive a higher modifier, while those above 100m receive a reduced modifier. This is a static factor based on location geography.

Contributing Factors

In addition to the numeric score, the system identifies qualitative contributing factors displayed in each location's detail popup:

  • Heavy recent rainfall — 24h rainfall exceeds 30mm
  • Sustained rainfall — 7-day rainfall exceeds 100mm
  • Saturated soil — Soil moisture above 65%
  • River above average — Discharge ratio exceeds 1.3x
  • Heavy forecast — 3-day forecast exceeds 60mm
  • Low elevation — Location below 15m elevation
  • Urban flooding risk — Dense urban area with impervious surfaces
  • Confluence of rivers — Located at major river junctions

Limitations and Disclaimers

Important: FloodKH is an experimental monitoring tool and should NOT be used as the sole basis for evacuation or emergency response decisions. Always follow official guidance from the Cambodia National Committee for Disaster Management (NCDM).
  • Spatial resolution: Data is gridded at 10–25 km resolution. Localized flooding from intense storms may not be captured.
  • Temporal lag: Satellite rainfall products have a 1–6 hour delay. Real-time ground observations are not incorporated.
  • River discharge: GloFAS provides modeled (not observed) discharge. Actual river levels may differ significantly.
  • Urban flooding: The system does not model urban drainage infrastructure, road flooding, or localized ponding.
  • Flash floods: Rapid-onset flash floods in mountainous areas may not be adequately predicted by this system.
  • Tonle Sap dynamics: The unique reverse-flow dynamics of Tonle Sap Lake are not explicitly modeled.
  • No ground truth: The system is not validated against actual flood extent observations and should be considered experimental.
Open Source: This project is open source and contributions are welcome. The scoring methodology can be improved with local knowledge, ground truth data, and more sophisticated hydrological modeling.
