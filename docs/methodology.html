Flood Risk Classification Methodology

FloodKH uses a deterministic, rule-based scoring system that combines multiple data sources into a composite risk score (0-100) for each of Phnom Penh's 14 districts.

Disclaimer: FloodKH is an independent monitoring tool for informational purposes only. It is NOT an official warning system. Always follow guidance from the National Committee for Disaster Management (NCDM) and local authorities.

5. District Vulnerability (max 15 pts)

Static score: drainage quality (0-12 pts) + flood history (0-3 pts) + low elevation bonus (+2 if <10m).

72-Hour Prediction

Future risk estimated by replacing observations with forecasts. Soil moisture extrapolated at +0.05/day (rising) or -0.03/day (falling). Confidence: High (T+24h), Medium (T+48h), Low (T+72h). Uncertainty widens 10% per step.

Limitations

  • District boundaries are approximate
  • Satellite sources currently simulated
  • Urban microflooding not captured
  • Predictions beyond 24h carry significant uncertainty
  • Not a replacement for official flood warnings
